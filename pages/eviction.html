Category: Cyber Defence Frameworks | Difficulty: Easy | Date: Apr 8, 2025

Unearth the monster from under your bed.

Introduction

In the "Eviction" room on TryHackMe, we explored the tactics and techniques of the Advanced Persistent Threat group, APT28. This exercise involved analyzing their methods using the MITRE ATT&CK framework to understand and mitigate potential threats.

Task: Understanding the Adversary

As a SOC analyst at E-corp, Sunny received intelligence about APT28 targeting similar organizations. Utilizing the MITRE ATT&CK Navigator, we identified the group's tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) to ensure the network's security.

Key Findings:

  • Reconnaissance and Initial Access: APT28 employs Spearphishing Links to perform reconnaissance and gain initial access.
  • Resource Development: They may compromise Email Accounts during resource development.
  • Execution Techniques: The group uses Malicious Files and Malicious Links to execute code.
  • Scripting Interpreters: Tools like PowerShell and Windows Command Shell are utilized for execution.
  • Persistence Mechanisms: Modifications to Registry Run Keys help maintain persistence.
  • Defense Evasion: Execution of system binaries like Rundll32 aids in evading defenses.
  • Discovery Techniques: Deployment of tools such as tcpdump indicates network sniffing activities.
  • Lateral Movement: Exploitation of SMB/Windows Admin Shares facilitates lateral movement.
  • Collection: Targeting information repositories like SharePoint suggests data collection efforts.
  • Command and Control: Utilization of External Proxy and Multi-hop Proxy techniques indicates attempts to exfiltrate data.

Conclusion

Through this room, we gained insights into APT28's methodologies and learned how to leverage the MITRE ATT&CK framework to detect and mitigate such threats effectively. This exercise underscores the importance of proactive threat intelligence and continuous monitoring in safeguarding organizational assets.
