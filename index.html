CTF Campaign

RootMexDGSE: Multi-Domain Intelligence Simulation

Launched on April 7, 2025, the RootMexDGSE CTF is a large-scale, month-long cyber challenge organized by Root-Me Pro and the DGSE as part of their official cyber officer recruitment campaign. This realistic, mission-based event engages players in tracking and neutralizing an emerging cyber threat: NullVastation, a mysterious digital adversary claiming responsibility for breaches in sensitive infrastructures.


Players are introduced to the threat via a chilling propaganda-style video and begin their investigation on a custom-built platform.


  • Artificial Intelligence: Analyzing malicious AI behavior, algorithmic logic, and adversarial data manipulation.
  • SOC Analysis: Interpreting logs and system calls to trace attacker activity, identify persistence, and correlate TTPs.
  • Digital Forensics: Performing artifact recovery, memory analysis, and timeline reconstruction from compromised systems.
  • Reverse Engineering: Dissecting binaries and malware samples to understand functionality and unpack obfuscation.
  • Web Security & Privilege Escalation: Exploiting vulnerable web apps, uploading shells, and elevating user privileges.
  • OSINT: Leveraging open-source data and metadata to investigate digital footprints and social engineering campaigns.

The challenge structure is open-ended, encouraging players to navigate between missions in any order (except for the final one, which remains locked until prerequisite objectives are completed). Each mission mirrors real-life cyber incidents and builds on actual investigation workflows, offering a highly immersive experience.


Focus: Threat tracking, log analysis, reverse shell decoding, persistence mechanisms, and critical thinking under pressure.

MSc Project

From Data to Justice: Enhancing Forensic Tool Reliability Through Standardized Testing

This project enhances digital forensic tool reliability by designing a standardized synthetic dataset that simulates real-world forensic scenarios, including deleted files, fragmented data, and metadata manipulation. By testing forensic tools across diverse file systems (APFS, EXT4, NTFS), it aims to identify performance inconsistencies and propose a framework for standardized testing, improving transparency and trust in digital forensic investigations.
